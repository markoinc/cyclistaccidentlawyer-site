## 2. KEY TAX RULES FOR KURIOS (2025/2026)

### Self-Employment Tax
- **Rate:** 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare)
- **On:** Net self-employment income (Schedule C profit)
- **Social Security cap:** $176,100 (2026)
- **Deduction:** 50% of SE tax is deductible on Schedule 1 (reduces AGI)
- **Estimated taxes:** Due quarterly (Apr 15, Jun 15, Sep 15, Jan 15)

### Standard Mileage Rate
- **2025:** $0.70/mile
- **2026:** $0.725/mile (72.5 cents)
- Must keep mileage log (date, destination, purpose, miles)

### Home Office Deduction
- **Simplified method:** $5/sq ft Ã— up to 300 sq ft = max $1,500
- **Regular method:** Actual expenses Ã— % of home used for business
- Requires **regular and exclusive** business use
- **Note:** Marko travels â€” home office only applies when he has a dedicated workspace

### Meals Deduction
- **50% deductible** for business meals with clients/prospects
- Must document: date, amount, who, business purpose
- **NOT deductible:** Personal meals, meals alone (unless traveling)

### Section 179 Deduction (2026)
- **Max deduction:** $1,250,000
- **100% bonus depreciation restored** (One Big Beautiful Bill Act, Jan 2025)
- Computers, equipment, software, vehicles can be fully deducted in year of purchase

### Health Insurance
- Self-employed can deduct 100% of health premiums
- Goes on Schedule 1, not Schedule C
- Includes medical, dental, long-term care
- **SafetyWing** travel health insurance: deductible as business insurance (Line 15)

### Retirement Plans (for future)
- **SEP-IRA:** 25% of net SE income, up to $70,000 (2026)
- **Solo 401(k):** $23,500 employee + 25% employer = up to $70,000 combined
- Reduces both income tax AND self-employment tax

## 5. BUSINESS vs PERSONAL EXPENSE RULES

### IRS Rule: "Ordinary and Necessary"
An expense must be:
1. **Ordinary** â€” Common and accepted in your trade/business
2. **Necessary** â€” Helpful and appropriate (not required to be indispensable)

### Common Gray Areas for KuriosBrand

| Expense | Business? | Rule |
|---------|-----------|------|
| T-Mobile phone | Partial | Deduct business % (e.g., 70%) |
| Internet | Partial | Deduct business % |
| Flights (Peru, Israel) | Depends | Business if primarily for business; personal days ok if trip is mainly business |
| Meta Ads on personal CC | Yes | Still business expense â€” just paid from wrong card |
| Spotify | Depends | Business if used for content creation; personal otherwise |
| Apple subscriptions | Depends | iCloud for business files = yes; Apple Music = no |
| Robinhood transfers | No | Personal investment, not business expense |
| ATM withdrawals abroad | Depends | Business if for business expenses; track what cash was used for |
| Student loans | No | Personal debt |
| Affirm payments | Depends | If purchased business equipment = yes |
| Discover CC | Depends | Only business charges are deductible |
| SafetyWing | Yes | Travel health insurance for business travel |
| Wise transfers | Depends | Business if paying for business expenses abroad |

### Mixing Personal & Business (Owner's Draws)
- Single-member LLC: No legal requirement for separate accounts
- **BUT** mixing can "pierce the corporate veil" (reduce LLC protection)
- Personal expenses paid from business account = **Owner's Draw** (not expense)
- Owner's draws are NOT tax deductible
- Best practice: Transfer money to personal account FIRST, then spend

## 6. CASH BASIS ACCOUNTING RULES

### When to Record Revenue
- **Cash basis:** When money hits your bank account
- Stripe deposits: Record on the day the payout lands in Chase 4991
- Zelle: Record on the day received
- NOT when the invoice is sent or work is done

### When to Record Expenses
- **Cash basis:** When money leaves your bank account
- CC charges: Record when the charge posts (NOT when you pay the CC bill)
- **CC payments are NOT expenses** â€” they're debt repayment
- Subscriptions: Record each monthly charge as it posts

### Stripe Revenue (Cash Basis)
- Gross charges â‰  Revenue (some fail, some get refunded)
- **Revenue = Net amount deposited to bank** (after fees, refunds, chargebacks)
- Stripe fees: Separate expense (Line 10 or Line 27a)
- Stripe Capital repayments: Principal = not deductible; Fee portion = deductible interest

### Credit Card Accounting
- CC charge = Expense (record when charge posts)
- CC payment from checking = NOT an expense (it's paying down debt)
- CC interest = Deductible if business card (Line 16b)
- **Double-counting risk:** Don't count both the CC charge AND the checking payment
