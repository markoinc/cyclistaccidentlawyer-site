### The Mom Test by Rob Fitzpatrick

**Core Thesis:** People lie to be nice. Learn to get truth instead.

**The 3 Rules:**
1. Talk about their life, not your idea
2. Ask about specifics in the past, not opinions about future
3. Talk less, listen more

**Good Questions:**
- "Tell me about the last time you had this problem..."
- "What have you tried to solve it?"
- "What happened when you tried X?"
- "How much does this problem cost you?"
- "What would you do if you couldn't solve this?"

**Bad Questions (get compliments, not truth):**
- "Would you use this?"
- "Is this a good idea?"
- "How much would you pay?"

**Key Insight:** Deflect compliments. When someone says "Great idea!" ask "Why? Tell me about your experience with this."

### Built to Sell by John Warrillow

**Core Thesis:** Build a business that can thrive without you, and you can sell for big multiples.

**8-Step Model:**
1. Specialize (don't generalize)
2. No client > 15% of revenue
3. Create Standard Service Offering (repeatable process)
4. Positive cash flow (charge upfront)
5. Build a sales team (prove replicability)
6. Stop custom work (stay focused)
7. Management incentive plan (retain key people)
8. 2+ years of financials (prove the model)

**Exit Multiples:**
- Service company: 1-3x revenue
- Productized company: 4-6x revenue
- SaaS: 6-10x+ ARR

**Key Insight:** "The true product of your business is the business itself."

**Owner Dependency Trap:** If they think you ARE the business, expect long earnouts and low multiples.

4. **"That's Right"** - The breakthrough phrase
5. **Calibrated Questions** - "How" and "What" questions
6. **Accusation Audit** - List negatives upfront to defuse
