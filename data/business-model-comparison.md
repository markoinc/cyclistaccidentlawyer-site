## 4. SCALABILITY

### Model A: PI Website + SEO

**Scaling pattern:** LINEAR with stepwise jumps

```
Solo (1-2/mo) → +PM hire (3-4/mo) → +Content/Dev hire (5-6/mo) → +Account Manager (7-8/mo)
```

- **Bottleneck 1:** Marko's personal selling bandwidth (limits to ~4-6 deals/mo without a closer)
- **Bottleneck 2:** Delivery quality (each additional person dilutes quality control)
- **Bottleneck 3:** Geographic exclusivity is a ceiling — 380 US metros, but only ~80-100 are worth $15K+
- **Hiring trigger:** First hire at client #4/month (project manager)
- **Revenue ceiling:** ~$4-5M/year without branching into other practice areas

**Scaling advantage:** Retainers compound. Once you have 30 retainer clients at $6K/mo, that's $180K/mo recurring regardless of new client acquisition. The website is the entry; retainers are the scale engine.

### Model B: MVA Lead Gen

**Scaling pattern:** EXPONENTIAL (with capital)

```
White label (5-10 clients) → Own fulfillment (10-20) → Multi-state (20-50) → Intake service (50+)
```

- **Bottleneck 1:** Ad spend capital (need to float $50K-$200K/mo in ads to scale)
- **Bottleneck 2:** Lead quality at scale (more volume = lower quality)
- **Bottleneck 3:** Client acquisition — PI lawyers are skeptical of lead gen vendors
- **Hiring trigger:** Intake specialist at 15+ clients, media buyer at $50K+/mo ad spend
- **Revenue ceiling:** $10M-$50M+ (lead gen scales with capital, not headcount)

**Scaling advantage:** Lead gen is fundamentally more scalable than services. Double ad spend → (roughly) double leads → double revenue. No geographic exclusivity ceiling. But requires significant capital.

### Verdict: Model B scales bigger; Model A scales more profitably

| | Model A | Model B |
|---|---|---|
| Scaling type | Linear (people-dependent) | Exponential (capital-dependent) |
| Revenue ceiling | ~$4-5M/yr | $10M-$50M+ |
| Profit ceiling | ~$1.5-2M/yr | $3-15M+/yr |
| Capital to scale | Low | Very High |
| Headcount to scale | High (1 per 3-4 clients) | Low (1 per 15-20 clients) |
