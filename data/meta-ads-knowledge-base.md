## Campaign Setup (Step-by-Step)

### The Modern Campaign Structure (2025-2026)

**Simplified Structure is Key:**
> "The biggest mistake I see is people overcomplicating their account structure. In 2024-2025, Meta's algorithm is so sophisticated that you want to give it maximum flexibility."

**Recommended Setup:**
1. **Campaign Level:**
   - Use Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns (ASC) for e-commerce
   - Use Sales objective for lead generation
   - Enable Campaign Budget Optimization (CBO) by default

2. **Ad Set Level:**
   - Start with broad targeting (no interest targeting)
   - Use Advantage+ Audience when possible
   - Set appropriate conversion events (Purchase, Lead, etc.)

3. **Ad Level:**
   - 3-6 creative variations per ad set
   - Mix formats: static images, videos, carousels
   - Use Flexible Ads feature for automatic optimization

### The 3-2-2 Structure
> "For testing, I recommend the 3-2-2 structure: 3 ad sets, 2 creatives per ad set, 2 different hooks or angles."

**Implementation:**
- Campaign 1: Testing (CBO, $50-100/day)
  - Ad Set 1: Broad targeting
  - Ad Set 2: Lookalike 1-3%
  - Ad Set 3: Interest stack
- Campaign 2: Scaling (CBO, scaled budget)
  - Winners only

### Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns (ASC)
> "ASC is the future. Meta wants you to use it. Give it all your creative assets and let the algorithm do the work."

**Setup Guidelines:**
- Upload 10-20+ creative variations
- Use existing customer list for exclusions (if needed)
- Set new customer budget cap at 70-80%
- Let it run for 7+ days before judging

## Budget & Scaling Strategies

### Starting Budget Framework

**For New Accounts:**
> "Start with $50-100/day. Don't try to scale immediately. You need data first."

- Day 1-3: $50/day, testing phase
- Day 4-7: Analyze data, pause losers
- Week 2: Increase to $100/day on winners
- Week 3+: Scale winners by 20-30% every 3 days

### The 20% Rule for Scaling
> "Never increase budget by more than 20% in a single day. The algorithm freaks out."

**Safe Scaling Protocol:**
1. Identify winning ad sets (ROAS > target for 3+ days)
2. Increase budget by 15-20%
3. Wait 3 days for learning phase
4. Repeat if performance holds

### Horizontal vs Vertical Scaling

**Vertical Scaling (Same Campaign):**
- Increase budget incrementally
- Safer but slower
- Best for stable performers

**Horizontal Scaling (Duplicate & Expand):**
> "Duplicate your winning ad set, put it in a new campaign with a fresh budget. This resets the learning phase and can unlock new audiences."

- Duplicate winning ads to new campaigns
- Test new audiences with proven creatives
- Run multiple campaigns simultaneously

### Budget Allocation by Funnel Stage

| Funnel Stage | Budget % | Objective |
|--------------|----------|-----------|
| TOF (Cold) | 60-70% | Traffic, Reach, Video Views |
| MOF (Warm) | 20-25% | Engagement, Leads |
| BOF (Hot) | 10-15% | Conversions, Sales |

### Daily Budget Minimums
> "Your daily budget should be at least 10x your target CPA. If your target CPA is $30, spend at least $300/day to exit learning phase quickly."

## Creative Testing Framework

### The Creative Testing Process

**Phase 1: Concept Testing**
> "Test the message before the creative. What hook resonates? What offer works? What angle converts?"

- Test 5-10 different hooks/angles
- Use simple static images
- Budget: $10-20 per concept
- Winner criteria: CTR > 1%, engagement positive

**Phase 2: Format Testing**
- Take winning concepts
- Create in multiple formats:
  - Static images (1:1, 4:5, 9:16)
  - Video (15s, 30s, 60s)
  - Carousel
  - Collection ads

**Phase 3: Iteration**
> "Once you find a winner, iterate on it 10 different ways. Change the hook, the CTA, the colors, the format."

### Creative Diversification Strategy
> "Your ads should look like native content, not ads. UGC, testimonials, behind-the-scenes, founder stories - these outperform polished brand ads."

**Creative Mix:**
- 40% UGC (User Generated Content)
- 30% Testimonials/Social Proof
- 20% Product/Offer Focused
- 10% Brand/Lifestyle

### Hook Testing Framework
> "The first 3 seconds of video determine everything. Test hooks relentlessly."

**Winning Hook Types:**
1. **Problem-Agitate:** "Tired of [problem]?"
2. **Curiosity:** "I can't believe this actually worked..."
3. **Social Proof:** "100,000+ people are using this..."
4. **Contrarian:** "Stop doing [common advice]"
5. **Direct Benefit:** "Get [result] in [timeframe]"

### The 3-30-3 Rule
> "You have 3 seconds to grab attention, 30 seconds to tell your story, and 3 seconds for the call to action."

## Audience Targeting (2026 Post-Andromeda)

### The Shift to Broad Targeting
> "Andromeda changed everything. Meta's AI is so good now that detailed targeting often hurts performance. Go broad and let the algorithm find your buyers."

**The New Targeting Hierarchy:**
1. **Broad (No Targeting)** - Best for established accounts with pixel data
2. **Advantage+ Audience** - Meta's AI-powered targeting
3. **Lookalikes** - Still effective, start with 1-3%
4. **Interest Stacking** - Use as fallback, not primary

### Advantage+ Audience Setup
> "Give Meta audience suggestions, not restrictions. Add interests as suggestions, not requirements."

**Best Practices:**
- Add 5-10 interest suggestions
- Don't use exclusions unless necessary
- Let Meta expand beyond suggestions
- Trust the algorithm with data

### Lookalike Audiences That Still Work

**High-Value Seed Audiences:**
1. Purchasers (Top 25% by value)
2. Repeat purchasers
3. High LTV customers
4. Email subscribers who purchased
5. Video viewers (75%+)

> "Your lookalike is only as good as your seed audience. Garbage in, garbage out."

**Lookalike Percentages:**
- 1% - Highest quality, smallest reach
- 1-3% - Sweet spot for most advertisers
- 3-5% - Broader, good for scaling
- 5-10% - Very broad, use for prospecting only

### Retargeting in 2026
> "Retargeting isn't dead, but it's different. Focus on engagement retargeting over pixel retargeting due to iOS changes."

**Effective Retargeting Pools:**
- Video viewers (25%, 50%, 75%, 95%)
- Page/profile engagers (30, 60, 90 days)
- Website visitors with Conversions API
- Customer lists (uploaded)

### First-Party Data is King
> "The advertisers winning in 2025-2026 have strong first-party data. Email lists, customer databases, CRM integrations - this is your competitive advantage."

## KPIs to Track

### Primary Metrics

| Metric | Target Range | Notes |
|--------|--------------|-------|
| ROAS | 3x+ (varies by margin) | Primary success metric |
| CPA | Industry dependent | Cost to acquire customer |
| CPM | $5-30 | Cost per 1000 impressions |
| CTR | 1-3% | Click-through rate |
| CVR | 2-5% | Conversion rate |

### Leading Indicators
> "Don't just look at ROAS. Watch the leading indicators that predict future performance."

**Key Leading Indicators:**
1. **Link CTR** - Should be >1% for cold traffic
2. **Hook Rate** - 3-second video views / impressions (aim for 30%+)
3. **Hold Rate** - ThruPlay / 3-second views (aim for 15%+)
4. **Outbound CTR** - Clicks that leave Facebook
5. **CPM Trends** - Rising CPM = audience fatigue

### Secondary Metrics
- Frequency (keep under 3 for TOF)
- Reach (unique people reached)
- Video average watch time
- Post engagement rate
- Landing page views vs clicks

### Attribution Windows
> "Use 7-day click, 1-day view as your default. Compare to 7-day click only for a cleaner picture."

**Recommended Settings:**
- Default: 7-day click, 1-day view
- Conservative: 7-day click only
- Testing: 1-day click for quick reads

### Breakeven ROAS Calculator
```
Breakeven ROAS = 1 / (Profit Margin %)

Example:
- Product Price: $100
- Cost of Goods: $30
- Profit Margin: 70%
- Breakeven ROAS: 1 / 0.70 = 1.43x
```

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake #1: Not Enough Creative Volume
> "You're not spending too much money. You're testing too few creatives. The brands winning are producing 20-50 new creatives per week."

**Fix:** Implement a creative production system. Aim for 10+ new creatives per week minimum.

### Mistake #2: Killing Ads Too Early
> "I see people turn off ads after 2 days. The learning phase takes 50 conversions. Give your campaigns time."

**Fix:** Wait for at least 50 conversions OR 7 days before making decisions.

### Mistake #3: Over-Segmenting Audiences
> "Stop creating 50 different ad sets for 50 different interests. You're fragmenting your data and confusing the algorithm."

**Fix:** Consolidate ad sets. Use broad targeting or Advantage+ Audience.

### Mistake #4: Ignoring Creative Fatigue
> "That winning ad won't win forever. Creative fatigue is real. Watch for rising CPMs and declining CTRs."

**Signs of Fatigue:**
- CTR declining over 3+ days
- CPM increasing while CTR drops
- Frequency above 3
- Comment sentiment turning negative

**Fix:** Refresh creatives every 2-4 weeks. Always have new creatives in testing.

### Mistake #5: Wrong Conversion Event
> "If you're optimizing for purchases but only getting 10/week, you don't have enough data. Move up the funnel."

**Conversion Event Ladder:**
- 50+ conversions/week â†’ Optimize for Purchase
- 25-50/week â†’ Optimize for Add to Cart
- <25/week â†’ Optimize for View Content or Leads

### Mistake #6: Poor Tracking Setup
> "If your Conversions API isn't set up properly, you're flying blind. iOS 14+ changes made this non-negotiable."

**Required Setup:**
- Facebook Pixel (base code)
- Conversions API (server-side)
- Event Match Quality > 6.0
- Aggregated Event Measurement configured

### Mistake #7: Ignoring Landing Page Experience
> "Your ads can be perfect, but if your landing page sucks, you'll never convert. The ad and landing page must be congruent."

**Landing Page Checklist:**
- Load time < 3 seconds
- Mobile optimized
- Message matches ad
- Clear CTA above fold
- Social proof visible

## Troubleshooting

### High CPM, Low Reach
**Possible Causes:**
- Audience too small
- Low relevance score
- Bidding too low
- Competitive auction

**Solutions:**
1. Expand audience size
2. Refresh creative
3. Test automatic bidding
4. Try different placements

### Low CTR (<1%)
**Possible Causes:**
- Weak hook/headline
- Creative doesn't stand out
- Wrong audience
- Offer not compelling

**Solutions:**
1. Test new hooks (first 3 seconds)
2. Try different creative formats
3. Broaden targeting
4. Improve offer/discount

### High CTR, Low Conversions
**Possible Causes:**
- Clickbait creative
- Landing page issues
- Audience mismatch
- Price objection

**Solutions:**
1. Align ad message with landing page
2. Check landing page speed
3. Review audience quality
4. Test different price points/offers

### Stuck in Learning Phase
> "If you're stuck in learning limited, you need more conversions. Either increase budget or optimize for an event higher up the funnel."

**Solutions:**
1. Increase daily budget (10x target CPA)
2. Consolidate ad sets
3. Move to higher-funnel event
4. Wait it out (up to 2 weeks)

### Account Restricted/Disabled
**Prevention:**
- Follow Facebook's policies strictly
- Avoid prohibited content categories
- Use proper disclaimers
- Don't make claims you can't prove

**Recovery:**
1. Request review through Ads Manager
2. Check Business Help Center
3. Contact Facebook support
4. Consider new Business Manager (last resort)

### Sudden Performance Drop
**Diagnostic Steps:**
1. Check for algorithm changes/updates
2. Review audience overlap
3. Analyze creative fatigue
4. Check competitor activity
5. Verify tracking is working

**Quick Fixes:**
- Launch new creative
- Duplicate winning campaigns
- Test new audiences
- Adjust bidding strategy
