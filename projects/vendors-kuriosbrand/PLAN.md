# vendors.kuriosbrand.com - Master Plan

## Vision
Authority SEO site that pairs PI law firms with trusted lead vendors.
Goal: 2-3 inbound calls/day on autopilot from PI attorneys seeking lead generation partners.

## Positioning
- **Angle**: "Find the right lead vendor for your law firm"
- **Value**: Unbiased reviews, comparisons, and matching service
- **Trust**: You're the connector, not just a vendor
- **SEO Play**: Rank for "best legal lead generation company", "[vendor] reviews", "PI lead gen comparison"

## Site Architecture

### Core Pages
1. **Home** - Hero: "Stop Wasting Money on Bad Leads. Find Your Perfect Match."
2. **Vendor Directory** - Database of lead gen companies with ratings, reviews, pricing
3. **Comparison Tool** - Side-by-side vendor comparisons
4. **Reviews** - Individual vendor review pages (SEO gold)
5. **Guides** - "How to Choose a Lead Vendor", "Red Flags to Avoid", etc.
6. **Blog** - Scraped topics from Reddit/forums, SEO content
7. **Quiz/Matcher** - "Find Your Ideal Vendor" interactive tool → captures lead

### Vendor Database Fields
- Company name
- Website
- Pricing model (per lead, media buy, retainer)
- Price range
- Lead types (MVA, PI, Mass Tort, etc.)
- States covered
- Minimum spend
- Contract terms
- Pros/Cons
- User reviews
- Our rating (1-5)
- Verified badge (for partners)

### SEO Content Strategy

#### Target Keywords
- "best personal injury lead generation companies"
- "legal lead gen reviews"
- "[Vendor Name] reviews" (all competitors)
- "how much do PI leads cost"
- "exclusive vs shared legal leads"
- "FindLaw alternatives"
- "Martindale vs Avvo vs [competitor]"
- "legal lead generation scams to avoid"

#### Content Types
1. **Vendor Reviews** - Deep dives on each company (scrape Reddit for real complaints)
2. **Comparison Posts** - "X vs Y: Which is Better for PI Firms?"
3. **Guides** - "Complete Guide to PI Lead Generation in 2026"
4. **Cost Breakdowns** - "How Much Should You Pay Per Lead?"
5. **Warning Posts** - "5 Red Flags When Choosing a Lead Vendor"
6. **Case Studies** - Success stories (use partner data)

### Automation Pipeline

#### Content Generation (Scraped Topics)
- Reddit monitoring → extract questions → generate articles
- Competitor monitoring → new vendors → add to database
- Social listening → pain points → content ideas

#### Reddit Commenting
- Find threads about lead gen, marketing for lawyers
- Comment with helpful info + link to relevant guide
- Build authority over time
- Managed accounts (later phase)

### Lead Capture
1. **Quiz/Matcher** - "What type of leads do you need?" → email capture
2. **Comparison Download** - "Get our full vendor comparison PDF" → email
3. **Calendly Embed** - "Talk to a Lead Specialist" → book call
4. **Exit Intent** - "Before you go - get our vendor red flags checklist"

### Tech Stack
- **Hosting**: Cloudflare Pages (free, fast, edge)
- **Framework**: Same as leads.kuriosbrand.com (React/Vite)
- **CMS**: Headless (Notion as backend? Or Contentful)
- **Database**: Supabase for vendor directory
- **Forms**: Supabase + webhook to CRM
- **Analytics**: Plausible or Fathom (privacy-focused)

### Branding
- Same color scheme as leads.kuriosbrand.com
- More CRO-optimized design
- Trust badges prominent
- Social proof (reviews, testimonials)
- Professional but approachable

## Revenue Model
1. **Primary**: Referral fees from partner vendors (CasesOnDemand, Patrick)
2. **Secondary**: "Verified Partner" badges (paid placement?)
3. **Tertiary**: Affiliate commissions from vendors
