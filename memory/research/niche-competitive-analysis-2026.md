## 4. NICHE SELECTION FRAMEWORKS

### The Blueprint Training Framework (from deep-dive article)
Three critical questions for evaluating niche profitability:

1. **Does your service directly result in leads/sales?** ‚Üí Website + SEO = bottom-of-funnel lead gen
2. **Is there significant demand for the client's offering?** ‚Üí Check search volume for "[service] near me"
3. **What is the value of a single customer?** ‚Üí Higher = easier to justify your fees

Their top picks: Men's Cosmetic/Elective Procedures, Home Services, Professional Services (mid-market).

### Josh Nelson / Seven Figure Agency Framework
**"Rule of Five Ones":**
1. **One target market** (single niche)
2. **One core offer** (your website + management package)
3. **One lead generation strategy** (Meta ads for you)
4. **One conversion mechanism** (your sales process)
5. **One-year commitment** to this focused strategy

**His niche selection criteria:**
- Check the marketing spend (do they invest in marketing?)
- Gauge the market size (aim for at least 10,000 businesses)
- Look for industry associations (networking goldmine)
- Spot the events (trade shows = sales opportunities)
- Follow your passion

Josh himself built his agency to $4.5M/yr ARR focusing on **Plumbing & HVAC**. He averages 5 new clients/month at $1,500/mo each. Home services are his #1 recommendation because they're "recession-resistant."

### Mitch Wiggins Framework
Mitch Wiggins (MitchWiggins.com) is a coaching business coach rather than a traditional agency niche coach. His framework focuses on:
- **Niche + Offer** combination (not just niche alone)
- **Content-led lead generation** (no paid ads, no cold DMs)
- **Organic client attraction** through content that sells
- Target: $10K-$25K/month coaching businesses

His approach is more coaching-business oriented than agency-specific, but the niche+offer principle applies: **Don't just pick a niche ‚Äî pair it with an irresistible offer that solves their #1 pain.**

For Marko's context, the Wiggins principle would be: "SEO-optimized website" is the niche offer, but the REAL offer should be framed as the business outcome: **"Get 20+ new customers per month from Google without paying for ads forever."**

### Combined Scoring Framework (My Synthesis)

**NICHE SCORE = (Customer LTV √ó Marketing Budget √ó Market Size) √∑ (Agency Competition √ó Sales Difficulty)**

| Factor | Weight | Why |

## 7. STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

### Pricing Position
Your $5K-$10K + $500/mo is priced in a **sweet spot** that's:
- **Above** DIY/template solutions ($0-$2K) ‚Üí You offer real value
- **Below** established niche agencies ($10K-$25K + $2K-5K/mo) ‚Üí You're the affordable expert
- **The "Goldilocks zone"** for business owners doing $500K-$5M in revenue

### Meta Ad Strategy by Niche
For each niche, the Meta ad creative should:
1. **Call out the niche** in the first 3 seconds ("Attention Pest Control Owners...")
2. **Show a before/after** of a terrible website vs. a beautiful one
3. **Name the outcome** ("Get 30+ calls per month from Google")
4. **Anchor the price against their current spend** ("You spend $3K/mo on ads that go to a $500 website...")

### Recommended Starting Niches (Pick 2 to Test)

**If you want fastest close rates:** Med Spas + Pest Control
- Med spa owners are marketing-savvy and will pay for aesthetics
- Pest control owners are practical ROI-focused buyers
